Contract analysis

We reviewed the 24-page Maplewood Senior Living Residency Agreement dated March 2026. The contract contains 3 significant red flags, 4 items to watch, and 3 clauses worth negotiating before signing.

The most serious issues are an uncapped rate escalation clause with no annual limit, a mandatory pre-dispute arbitration waiver in Exhibit D, and a rapid discharge provision giving the facility broad discretion to remove a resident with as few as 5 days notice.

Red flag
Uncapped rate escalation — no annual limit
Article IV, Sec. B
"The Facility reserves the right to adjust the Monthly Service Fee upon thirty (30) days prior written notice to Resident or Resident's Representative. Rate adjustments are not limited in frequency or amount and may occur at any time."

This clause gives Maplewood unlimited ability to raise your monthly rate — with only 30 days warning and no cap on how much or how often. A family paying $4,200/month today could legally be charged $6,500/month within a year with no recourse other than moving out.

Industry context: Responsible facilities cap annual increases at CPI + 2–3%, or a fixed percentage such as 5% per year. This contract has no such protection.

Ask for: A written addendum capping annual rate increases at the lesser of 5% or CPI for the Illinois metro area. Many facilities will agree, particularly if occupancy is below capacity.
Red flag
Mandatory pre-dispute arbitration waiver
Article IX, Sec. A — Exhibit D
"Any and all disputes, controversies or claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively by binding arbitration... Resident and Facility expressly waive any right to trial by jury or judge."

By signing as written, your family permanently gives up the right to sue Maplewood in court — including the right to join a class action lawsuit. All disputes are decided by a private arbitrator, not a judge or jury.

Why this matters: In 2024, Sunrise Senior Living settled a class action for $18.2 million. Families who had signed the arbitration waiver were excluded from the settlement entirely.

Ask for: Removal of Exhibit D before signing. If the facility refuses, request that it be optional and that you have 30 days after signing to withdraw in writing. Consult an elder law attorney on your specific Illinois rights.
Red flag
Broad discharge authority — 5-day notice
Article VII, Sec. C.2
"The Facility may terminate this Agreement and discharge Resident upon five (5) days written notice in the event of behaviors determined by Facility management, in its sole discretion, to pose a risk to the safety, well-being, or comfort of other residents or staff."

The phrase "in its sole discretion" means Maplewood — not an independent reviewer, not a physician — decides whether a behavior is a "risk to comfort." That standard is vague enough to justify almost any discharge decision. Five days is also an extremely short window to arrange alternative placement for a vulnerable adult.

Ask for: Amendment requiring (1) a licensed professional's written clinical assessment before behavioral discharge, (2) a minimum 30-day notice period, and (3) a documented appeals process.
Watch
Level of care increases — facility-determined, no appeal
Article IV, Sec. D
"Resident's level of care shall be assessed by Facility nursing staff on a quarterly basis or more frequently as clinically indicated. Level of care tier and associated fees may be adjusted at the Facility's discretion based on assessment findings."

The facility's own staff conducts the assessments that determine how much you pay. No third-party review or appeals process is described anywhere in this contract.

Ask: What is the process for requesting an independent review if we disagree with a care level assessment? Get the answer in writing as a signed addendum.
Watch
Full payment required during hospitalization
Article IV, Sec. F
"Resident shall remain financially responsible for the full Monthly Service Fee during any temporary absence from the Facility, including but not limited to hospitalization, rehabilitation, or skilled nursing transfer."

If your parent is hospitalized for 3 weeks, you pay full rent at Maplewood for all 3 weeks — simultaneously with hospital costs not covered by Medicare. Some facilities offer a reduced bed hold rate of 50–75% during stays over 7 days. This contract offers none.

Ask for: A reduced bed hold rate — typically 50–75% of monthly fee — for hospital stays exceeding 7 days.
Watch
Ancillary supply markup — cost plus 15%
Article V, Sec. B — Exhibit C
"Supplies and materials provided by the Facility beyond those included in the base service rate, including incontinence products, wound care materials, and medical supplies, shall be billed at the Facility's cost plus fifteen percent (15%)."

A 15% markup is higher than the 10% we consider standard. For a resident requiring regular incontinence and wound care, ancillary costs can reach $300–400/month — meaning the extra 5% markup adds $15–20/month over industry standard.

Ask for: Monthly itemized statements for all ancillary charges, and consider requesting the markup be reduced to 10% or that you be allowed to purchase supplies through an outside pharmacy.
Watch
Community fee — $3,000, non-refundable after 14 days
Article III, Sec. A
"A non-refundable Community Fee of Three Thousand Dollars ($3,000) is due upon execution of this Agreement. After the fourteen (14) day rescission period, this fee is fully earned and non-refundable under all circumstances."

Fourteen days is a short window to decide whether this facility is the right fit. The $3,000 amount is also on the higher end of the typical $500–$3,500 range. Once the window closes, this money is gone regardless of outcome.

Ask for: Extension of the rescission period to 30 days, and/or a reduction of the community fee. Both are commonly negotiated for private-pay residents.
Negotiate
Medication management fee — $180/month
Article V, Sec. A — Exhibit C, Line 4
"Medication management services, including storage, administration, and documentation, shall be provided at an additional monthly fee of One Hundred Eighty Dollars ($180) per month."

$180/month is at the high end of the typical $75–$200 range. This fee is listed as standard but is frequently reduced for residents who self-administer most medications and require minimal staff involvement.

Ask: Whether the medication management fee can be reduced given the resident's level of independence with medications.

Negotiation guide

These are specific requests to make — in priority order — before signing. They are reasonable, commonly accepted requests that families successfully negotiate every day. Approach the admissions coordinator calmly and confirm any agreed changes in writing as a signed addendum.

1
Request removal of the arbitration agreement (Exhibit D)
"We'd like to discuss Exhibit D — the arbitration agreement. We're not comfortable waiving our right to court access. Can we proceed without signing it, or is there a version that makes it optional?"
2
Request a written annual rate increase cap
"We noticed the agreement doesn't limit annual rate increases. We'd like to add an addendum capping increases at 5% per year or CPI, whichever is lower. Is that something the facility can accommodate?"
3
Request a reduced bed hold rate during hospitalization
"We'd like to understand the policy if our parent needs to be hospitalized. Is there a reduced hold rate for stays beyond 7 days, rather than the full monthly fee?"
4
Request an independent appeals process for care assessments
"If we ever disagree with a care level assessment, what is the process for requesting an independent review? We'd like that process documented in the agreement."
5
Ask about the inspection record directly
"We noticed some medication-related citations in the state inspection history from 2024 and 2026. Can you walk us through what happened and what changes were made as a result?"
