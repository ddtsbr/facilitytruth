1
You do this
Prepare your contract for upload

You'll need a copy of the assisted living residency agreement — the document the facility has asked you to sign. Most contracts run between 15 and 30 pages. If you have a digital PDF already, you're ready. If you only have a paper copy, scan it using your phone.

We accept PDF files and photos (JPG or PNG). If uploading photos, one image per page works best. Our system processes both clean digital PDFs and phone-scanned documents.

How to scan a paper contract with your phone
Both iPhone and Android have built-in document scanners that produce clean, searchable PDFs in seconds. No app download required.
i
iPhone: Open the Files app → tap the Browse tab → tap the three-dot menu → Scan Documents. Or use Notes app → new note → camera icon → Scan Documents.
A
Android: Open Google Drive → tap the + button → Scan. The Drive scanner automatically flattens pages and creates a multi-page PDF.
Scan in a well-lit space and keep the pages flat. Lay each page on a table rather than holding it — this produces significantly cleaner results.

4
We do this
We pull the facility's inspection record

Simultaneously with the contract analysis, we query Medicare's Care Compare database — the federal government's public record of every Medicare-certified assisted living and nursing facility in the country. We retrieve the facility's star ratings, staffing ratios, health inspection scores, and any penalty or payment denial history.

We also pull available state health department inspection reports for your facility, where accessible, and summarize the findings in plain English. State survey reports are the most detailed public record of what actually happens inside a facility day-to-day — but they run 40 to 80 pages of regulatory language. We translate them.

Quick answers

The questions we hear most often before purchase.

Is this legal advice?
No. FacilityTruth provides informational analysis — we explain what contract language means and what it has meant for other families in similar situations. We do not tell you what your legal rights are or what legal action to take. For complex situations, we always recommend consulting a qualified elder law attorney. We'll tell you exactly when that's warranted, and point you to a directory to find one.
What if I've already signed the contract?
A report is still useful. Understanding what's in a signed contract helps you know your rights, anticipate future issues, and have informed conversations with the facility when problems arise. If you signed within the last 14 days, you may still be within the rescission period — your report will tell you exactly what that means for your specific contract.
What if my facility isn't Medicare-certified?
Some smaller or newer facilities are not in the Medicare database. If that's the case, your report will still include the full contract analysis and any available state inspection data — just without the Medicare star rating component. We'll note clearly what data was and wasn't available, and why.
What if my contract is handwritten or hard to read?
Standard printed contracts — even phone-scanned ones — process reliably. Heavily handwritten addenda or very poor quality scans may result in partial analysis, in which case your report will note which sections couldn't be read clearly. If you're concerned about quality, email us a sample page before purchasing and we'll tell you whether it's processable.
My state isn't on your list yet. Can I still order?
Yes. The contract analysis and Medicare facility data are available nationally. State inspection report summaries are currently available for CA, TX, FL, NY, PA, IL, OH, GA, NC, and MI. If your facility is in another state, your report will include everything except the state survey summary, and we'll note that clearly. More states are added regularly.
