The problem

What families don't know when they sign

Most families sign under emotional stress, with little time to review, and no one in their corner. The contract is written by lawyers who work for the facility.

Unlimited rate increases

Most contracts let facilities raise monthly rates with just 30 days notice — no cap on amount or frequency.

Level of care triggers

Facilities can unilaterally decide your parent needs more — and more expensive — care, with no independent review.

Arbitration waivers

Many contracts require you to give up the right to sue. Sunrise Senior Living paid $18.2M to families who didn't notice this clause.

Hidden ancillary fees

Medication management, wound care, supplies — marked up and billed separately with little disclosure upfront.

Rapid discharge clauses

Facilities can discharge a resident in as few as 3 days for "behavioral" reasons vague enough to cover almost anything.

Pay during hospitalization

Many contracts require full payment even during weeks-long hospital stays, with no hold rate or reduction offered.
